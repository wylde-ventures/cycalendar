By modeling the rhythmic cycles of nature the Lunar Cycle Calendar inspires harmonic scheduling of life’s events.

The Lunar Cycle Calendar inverts the contextual perspective of time. It subverts the standard western months and dates into the context of the lunar cycle rather than the other way around.

When you start scheduling in this way your sense of where you are in time becomes re-oriented to the lunar - and other - natural cycles.

Ducktaped Clocks
Jeremiah Konkle

I’ve found that I become the most alive and present when I am outside in nature.

When I'm out there long enough my body starts to forget the hustle and bustle of modern society.

And when that happens I feel a deep sense of relaxation and well being.

Because of this I’m constantly experimenting with ways to bring this sense of presence and aliveness into my daily home life.

Now, I’ve tried a lot of things. I tend to blame computers and electric gizmos and email and traffic and walls.

But one unexpected experiment has recently altered my perspective and surfaced a pivotal piece of the puzzle.

I tried to remove clocks and all other instances of “clock-time” from my life for two weeks.

It wasn't as easy as I'd thought. I turned clocks off. I duck-taped over clocks I couldn’t turn off.

I tried not to look at the timestamps on text messages or emails. If I had to meet someone I set an alarm.

I was expecting or hoping for my general level of stress to go down and my sense of well-being to go up. It was the opposite.

My anxiety started to go way up.

I didn’t know when to go to sleep for the night. I didn’t know when to eat. I didn’t know when to go for a walk, or read a book, or to work.

Because there was no when. There was only now.

That’s when I realized that this was a critical difference between life in the deep woods and life at home.

The nature of clocktime and modern calendars is strangely out of sync with natural rhythms.

Over the course of the two week experiment I was able to re-orient questions like “when should I go to sleep” into the only question I could answer:

"what do I want or need to do now?”

The answer to that question was found in the awareness of the natural rhythms within and around me.

I could start to feel the pulse of tiredness and alertness in myself but also in the world around me.

The pulse of hunger and satiation. The pulse of warmth and coolness. The pulses of my own needs and the pulses of the needs of others.

In the meetings of these pulses was the emergence of a life lived.

At the end of the two weeks the reality of coordinating my life with other people won out over continuing the experiment.

But I was hooked.

What if I could find a way to schedule “clock-time” and keep it “contained” within an awareness of and orientation to the natural rhythms and pulses of myself and the world.

This calendar is an answer to that question.

The Lunar Cycle Calendar’s primary orienting rhythm and pulse is the Synodic Month

A Synodic Month is a complete Lunar Phase Cycle, from new moon through the first quarter, full moon, last quarter, and returning to new moon.

The Lunar Cycle Calendar also orients to the rhythm and pulse of daily cycles, weekly cycles, and yearly cycles.

All of these cycles are correlated with each other through the use of the color wheel.

This allows us to notice the correlations in "felt sense" of morning, the waxing moon, and spring. Of midday, the full moon, and summer. And of evening, the waning moon, and autumn. And of night, the new moon, and winter.

When we start to orient to these naturaly occuring pulses and rhythms of energy and experience we start to see them everywhere.

As cliche as it sounds, this pulse is nothing more, or less, than the great circle of life. It is birth and death. It is life and compost.

The rhythms of the Sun and Moon are the syncopated metronomes in the symphony of life.
